Gender, culture and society

When Women Make Headlines

This visual essay was written along with Leonardo Nicoletti in collaboration with The Pudding. It analyzes 342,000 English headlines tagged with keywords synonymous with 'women' published in the top 50 English dailies in South Africa, UK, USA and India between 2005 and 2021. The data collected is publicly available on Github and the essay tells a story of some of the most interesting findings.

The need to represent: How AI can help counter gender disparity in the news

This community workshop I was a part of, was conducted by POLiS, LSE's media think-tank in collaboration with Google News. It brought together work done by journalists and data scientists from three different countries and discussed the challenges and methods in which data can be used to study represetation of womxn in the news.

The rise of political cyber troops in India has allowed disinformation to harness the power of the internet to influence citizens. This virtual escalation must be stopped to avoid creating a culture of mistrust and dishonesty. This essay presents arguments supporting why such a culture must be stopped. It was written along with Ignasi Cortes Arbues.

Responding to Imperial Blue's 'Men will be Men' ad campaign in India

This is an open letter to the marketing managers of Imperial Blue in India after the release of their "Men will be Men" ad campaign in 2020. The letter calls out the casual misgyny in an Indian context and was co-authored along with Sneha Kanukolanu. The letter, sent as an email to the management, never received a respone.

Water and disaster risk management

Water Justice: Operationalizing stability and fairness in transboundary water resource allocation

This is a methodological research project done for my MSc thesis. It uses multi-objective optimization, game theory and welfare economics to propose a method to identify just water distribution policies between states that share a water body. The case study was the Susquehanna River basin in the United States.

How the turn tables: Estimating spatio-temporal impact of Non-Pharmaceutical Interventions against COVID-19 in a large scale artifical city

Health Emergency Response in Interconnected Systems (HERoS) is a Horizon 2020 project funded by the European Commission to to support decision-makers in managing the Coronavirus outbreak. This research was about informing the city-level government in their COVID-19 response. My part was done in collaboration predominantly with Mikhail Sirenko and Jin Rui Yap and the rest of HERoS team at the Resilience Lab, TU Delft.

Storytelling

Same Same, But Different DISC Radio Season Two

This is a collaborative storytelling project which brought to life Connor McMullen's idea for World Storytelling Day 2022. Six of us came together to write a story that started from a blank canvas and developed into 6 different stories that use the same structure. We later recorded the stories as a podcast with the Dutch International Storytelling Centre
